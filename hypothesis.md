# Pre-registration of paper claims (qlnn)

**Status:** PRE-REGISTRATION — committed 2026-05-17 before downstream
experiments (Steps 4 and 5; QWGAN-GP synthetic-data lift and effective-
dimension expressivity).
**Project:** Quantum-Liquid Neural ODE for bioreactor OD modeling.
**Dataset:** qZETA bioreactor, 778 samples, single fermentation run.

This document is the pre-registration. The git commit timestamp that adds
this file to the repository is the pre-registration time. Any results
generated by code committed *after* this file are bound by the thresholds
below. Any deviation will be disclosed in a "deviations from pre-
registration" section in the paper.

## Scope of claims

This paper restricts its claims to **within-run extrapolation on a single
fermentation trajectory**. It does NOT claim:
- population-level generalization across fermentation runs,
- generalization across organisms or process conditions,
- quantum advantage on physical quantum hardware (the QLNN is run on
  classical statevector simulators — PennyLane `default.qubit` JAX —
  throughout).

All claims are relative to the locked evaluation protocol below.

## Locked evaluation protocol (already in effect)

This section is a restatement of the protocol that is already implemented
in code and used for the Phase B / Phase C reruns. It is reproduced here
so that the pre-registration is self-contained.

- Dataset: `data/raw/qZETA_data_copy.csv`, 778 rows, time-ordered.
- Splits: chronological 70 / 15 / 15 (train / val / test). No shuffling
  across time.
- History window: 24 steps, stride 1.
- OD scaling: **train-only** MinMax (the R3 leak fix), predictions clipped
  at `od_phys_max = 3.8` (a domain prior on bioreactor carrying capacity,
  not a test-derived bound).
- Other-feature scaling: train-only MinMax per feature.
- Metrics, all in raw OD units except `MSE_norm`:
  - `MAE_raw`, `RMSE_raw`, `R²_raw`,
  - `MSE_norm` (the selection metric),
  - `ΔOD_R²_raw` (R² on the residual against persistence — exposes the
    persistence floor; persistence is by construction 0).
- Seeds: `{0, 1, 2, 3, 4}` for both classical and QLNN (n=5).
- Aggregation: report mean and 95% confidence interval from the
  t-distribution, `t_{α/2, n-1} × std(ddof=1) / √n`. With n=5 the
  multiplier is 2.776.
- Selection: best val `MSE_norm` checkpoint per seed.
- Statistical test for head-to-head: **paired bootstrap over test
  windows**, n_iter = 10000, two-sided p-value computed as
  `2 * min(P(diff > 0), P(diff < 0))`. Significance threshold p < 0.05.

The paired bootstrap is implemented in
`src/quantum_liquid_neuralode/evaluation/bootstrap.py` and uses the
Efron & Tibshirani (1993) convention.

## Three claims (binding)

### Claim 1 — Synthetic data lift (HEADLINE)

**Pre-registered hypothesis.** Training a forecasting model on real +
QWGAN-GP-synthesized OD trajectories reduces test MAE on the 3-h-ahead
forecast task relative to training on real data alone, by a margin that
is statistically significant under a paired bootstrap over test windows
(two-sided p < 0.05).

**Primary endpoint.** Test MAE at horizon **h = 3** (the discriminating
regime identified by the Phase B horizon ablation — see
`results/horizon_sweep_table/horizon_sweep_table.md`, where persistence
test R² collapses from 0.9052 at h=1 to −0.0371 at h=3). Specifically:

```
Δ_MAE = MAE(real-only training) − MAE(real + synthetic) > 0
        with paired bootstrap two-sided p < 0.05 over the 71 h=3 test
        windows, n_iter = 10000, seed-pooled.
```

Training-set construction for both arms:
- Real-only: the 472 h=3 training windows.
- Real+synthetic: the 472 real windows plus K synthetic windows drawn
  from QWGAN-GP, with K = 472 (1:1 augmentation). K is fixed here and
  will not be tuned on the test set.

Both arms use the same downstream forecaster (the Phase-B classical
Liquid-ODE at `hidden_size = 4`, the param-matched variant — see
Claim 2) and the same training budget. The QWGAN-GP itself is trained
on the 472 h=3 training windows only; test windows are never visible
to the generator.

**Secondary endpoint.** Trajectory realism: DTW between QWGAN-GP-
generated trajectories and the test-set true trajectories must satisfy
both
- *absolute:* `DTW(QWGAN-GP, real_test) < 0.5` on train-only-MinMax-
  normalized OD,
- *relative:* `DTW(QWGAN-GP, real_test) < DTW(classical-WGAN-GP,
  real_test) − 0.1` (a classical-WGAN-GP baseline trained on the same
  data, same parameter count where possible).

Both reported as mean ± 95% CI over test trajectories.

**Null-result handling.** A pre-registered null result IS publishable
and will be reported as such. If we observe `Δ_MAE ≤ 0` or `p ≥ 0.05`
on the primary endpoint, we report the negative result in the headline
table and discuss why. We will not pivot to a different endpoint
(different horizon, different metric, different fraction) to recover a
positive claim. If the secondary endpoint passes while the primary
fails, the paper will state that the QWGAN-GP produced realistic
trajectories that did not translate into a downstream forecasting lift.

**Power analysis.** With n_test = 71 windows at h=3 and the observed
Phase-B std of test MAE at h=3 ≈ 0.025 (from
`results/baseline_classical_*/seeds_summary.json` h=3 rows), the
paired-bootstrap power to detect a true MAE reduction of Δ at α = 0.05
two-sided is approximately:
- Δ = 0.025 (1σ): ≈ 50% power,
- Δ = 0.015: ≈ 65–80% power (depends on per-window correlation),
- Δ = 0.010: < 50% power.

Effects below ~0.012 OD are underpowered on this dataset. We will report
the bootstrap CI alongside the point estimate so that an underpowered
null can be distinguished from a powered null.

### Claim 2 — Expressivity at matched parameter count

**Pre-registered hypothesis.** At **matched parameter count** (within
a factor of 1.5), the QLNN achieves a higher normalized effective
dimension on the locked training data than the classical Liquid-ODE.

**Primary endpoint.** Normalized effective dimension `d_norm` following
Abbas, Sutter, Zoufal, Lucchi, Figalli, Woerner, "The power of quantum
neural networks," *Nature Computational Science* **1**, 403–409 (2021),
Eq. (4). Concretely:

```
d_norm(γ, n) = (1/d) × log₂(
    (1/V_Θ) ∫_Θ √det( I_d + (γ·n / (2π·log n)) · F̂(θ) ) dθ
) × ( 2 log n / (γ n) )
```

where d is the number of trainable parameters, V_Θ is the parameter-
volume normalizer, F̂(θ) is the normalized empirical Fisher information
matrix at θ, n is the sample size, and γ ∈ (0, 1] is the Abbas
risk-aversion parameter (we report at γ = 1 for headline; γ ∈
{0.1, 0.5, 1.0} in supplementary).

**Estimator.** Empirical Fisher
`Î_F(θ) = (1/n) Σᵢ (∇_θ log p_θ(yᵢ|xᵢ)) (∇_θ log p_θ(yᵢ|xᵢ))ᵀ`,
evaluated at the trained parameters of each seed. The model output is
treated as a Gaussian with fixed variance σ² (estimated from training
residuals), in which case `∇_θ log p_θ(y|x) = (1/σ²) (y − μ_θ(x))
∇_θ μ_θ(x)`. The Jacobian of the model mean prediction w.r.t.
parameters is computed via JAX `jax.jacfwd` (small parameter count →
forward-mode is fastest).

**Sample size.** `n = 500` training windows, sampled deterministically
(seed 0) from the locked train split. We report `d_norm` at one
n-value for the headline (n = 500) and a sample-size sweep n ∈
{50, 100, 200, 500} in supplementary as a sanity check (see below).

**Parameter matching.** The QLNN has approximately 100 trainable
parameters (4 qubits × 3 layers × 3 angles per Rot block + encoder
projection + cell heads — exact count to be recorded in
`provenance.json` of the run that produces the table). The classical
counterpart is the param-matched-classical-sweep row at
`hidden_size = 4`, defined by
`configs/param_sweep/baseline_euler_h3_hidden4.yaml`, which has 86
trainable parameters. The ratio is 100/86 ≈ 1.16, within the factor-of-
1.5 matching tolerance committed here.

**Acceptance threshold.** `d_norm(QLNN) > d_norm(classical)`, with the
difference exceeding **1 unit** on the Abbas et al. (2021) scale (the
margin used as "meaningful" in that paper's headline figures). The
difference is reported as a paired comparison across the 5 seed pairs
(QLNN seed s vs. classical seed s), with 95% CI from the t-distribution
on the n=5 paired deltas.

**Sanity check.** `d_norm` should increase monotonically (or at least
non-decreasingly) with n for an over-parameterized model. We will
verify and report this curve **before** claiming the headline. If the
curve is non-monotone for either model, we treat the methodology as
not yet validated on this task and report the d_norm comparison as
exploratory rather than confirmatory.

**Null-result handling.** Same as Claim 1: a pre-registered null
(no significant d_norm gap, or d_norm gap below 1 unit) is publishable
and will be reported as the headline expressivity result.

**Caveats acknowledged here.** The Abbas et al. (2021) effective-
dimension framework has been critiqued in subsequent literature
(notably regarding whether the result generalizes beyond small
synthetic tasks; see e.g. Qian et al. 2024 and 2025 follow-ups). We
report `d_norm` as defined in Abbas et al. and explicitly do not
generalize the expressivity claim beyond this dataset and this
parameter range.

### Claim 3 — Sample efficiency

**Pre-registered hypothesis.** The QLNN reaches a target test MAE at
h = 3 with **fewer training windows** than the param-matched
classical model.

**Primary endpoint.** Data-fraction sweep at training fractions
**f ∈ {10, 25, 50, 100}%** of the 472 h=3 training windows. The
fraction is taken **chronologically from the start** of the training
segment (so the QLNN at f=10% sees the first 47 windows, never any
later ones — this preserves the temporal split and prevents inadvertent
leakage from late training windows that are closer in time to the test
segment).

For each (model, fraction) cell, we report test MAE (mean ± 95% CI
from t-distribution across 5 seeds).

Headline statistic: let `X = MAE(classical, f=100%)` (the classical
ceiling). Then `f*(QLNN) = min { f ∈ {10, 25, 50, 100}% :
MAE(QLNN, f) ≤ X }`. We report `f*(QLNN)` and the analogous
`f*(classical)`.

**Acceptance threshold.** `f*(QLNN) ≤ 50%` while `f*(classical) =
100%`, i.e., QLNN reaches the classical's full-data MAE with at most
half the data.

**Null-result handling.** Reported. If QLNN never reaches X, that is
the published result (`f*(QLNN) = ∞` in the table).

**Caveat.** The four-point fraction grid is coarse. We pre-commit not
to refine the grid post-hoc; if a tighter threshold is desired in
revision, we will declare it explicitly as exploratory.

## Methodology bindings

### Effective dimension

- **Definition:** Abbas et al. (2021) Eq. (4), normalized over the
  parameter volume `V_Θ` as described in that paper.
- **Estimator:** empirical Fisher
  `Î_F = (1/n) Σᵢ ∇_θ log p_θ(yᵢ|xᵢ) ∇_θ log p_θ(yᵢ|xᵢ)ᵀ`,
  evaluated at the trained parameters (one Fisher matrix per seed).
- **Gaussian-output regression reduction:** for fixed-σ Gaussian
  likelihood, this reduces to `Î_F = (1/(n σ²)) J^T J` where
  `J ∈ ℝ^{n × d}` is the per-sample Jacobian of the mean prediction
  w.r.t. θ. Implemented via `jax.jacfwd` on the QLNN side. The
  classical side computes the analogous Jacobian via PyTorch
  `torch.autograd.functional.jacobian` or per-sample reverse-mode
  loops.
- **σ estimate:** sample standard deviation of the model's training
  residuals at the trained parameters (recorded in `provenance.json`).
- **Sample size:** n = 500 training windows (headline);
  n ∈ {50, 100, 200, 500} sample-size curve in supplementary.
- **Numerical conditioning:** Î_F may be near-singular. We add a
  jitter of `1e-8 · trace(Î_F)/d` to the diagonal before the
  determinant computation, and report whether the jitter affects the
  reported value to two significant figures.

### Paired bootstrap (head-to-head model comparison)

- **Implementation:** `src/quantum_liquid_neuralode/evaluation/bootstrap.py`.
- **Resampling unit:** per-window residual pairs (model_A, model_B)
  on the same test window.
- **n_iter:** 10000.
- **p-value convention:** two-sided,
  `p = 2 · min(P_boot(diff > 0), P_boot(diff < 0))`, clipped to ≤ 1
  (Efron & Tibshirani 1993, *An Introduction to the Bootstrap*).
- **Seed handling — pre-committed:** **pooled-window bootstrap.** We
  concatenate per-seed (model_A_pred, model_B_pred, y_true) over all
  5 seeds into a single 5n_test-row pool and resample with replacement
  from that pool. The same window from a different seed counts as a
  distinct row. This maximizes power (n_test = 71 windows × 5 seeds
  = 355 paired residuals at h=3) and matches the convention in
  `bootstrap.py`. We do *not* compute per-seed bootstrap + Stouffer's
  Z combination; the pooled version is the headline.

### DTW

- **Library:** `dtaidistance` (preferred) or `tslearn.metrics.dtw`
  (fallback) — to be locked at first use.
- **Constraint:** no Sakoe-Chiba band (full DTW). Window-size
  restrictions sensitive on short sequences; we eat the O(n²) cost.
- **Distance metric:** Euclidean per-step on train-only-MinMax-
  normalized OD scalars.
- **Reporting:** mean ± 95% CI over test trajectories, using the
  t-distribution on the per-trajectory DTW values.

## What we are NOT claiming

- We do not claim QLNN > classical at h = 1. The Phase-B horizon
  ablation establishes that at h = 1 both models collapse onto
  persistence (test R² 0.9052 for persistence, 0.9048 ± 0.0161 for
  Liquid-ODE Euler — within noise). The h = 1 row remains in the
  paper as a sanity-floor baseline, not as a discriminating result.
- We do not claim cross-fermentation-run generalization.
- We do not claim quantum advantage on quantum hardware. The QLNN
  runs on classical statevector simulators throughout.
- We do not claim that physics-informed regularization always
  helps; we report it as an ablation under symmetric treatment
  (both QLNN +physics and classical +physics).
- We do not claim a specific QFI bound; effective dimension as
  defined above is the only expressivity-style claim.

## Provenance bindings

Every numerical result reported in the paper must come from a run
whose artifact directory contains a `provenance.json` recording:
- `git_commit` — the public-repo commit at training time,
- `git_dirty` — whether the working tree was clean,
- `data_sha256` — SHA-256 of `data/raw/qZETA_data_copy.csv`,
- `torch_version`, `jax_version`, `pennylane_version`,
- `platform`.

The paper will cite the **pre-registration commit** (the commit
introducing this file) as the pre-registration anchor.

## Deviations log

Any deviation from the bindings above discovered at paper-writing
time will be disclosed in a "Deviations from pre-registration"
section in the paper, including:
- what was changed,
- why,
- whether the deviation was discovered before or after observing the
  affected number.

The bar for declaring a deviation is low: even cosmetic changes
(e.g., switching DTW library) will be logged.
