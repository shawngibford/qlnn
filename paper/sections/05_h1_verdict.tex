\subsection{Sign-flip behavior across the layered points}
\label{sec:verdict-signflip}

The verdicts split into three groups by point-estimate sign.

\textbf{Group A (P7.5 raw, P7.6 default-Adam, P7.6 HPO-best):
modest positive $\ddiff$.} Point estimates in $[+0.03, +0.11]$, with
only P7.5's sample-size-fragile $n=9$ value excluding zero. Doubling
the sample from $n=9$ to $n=18$ moves the estimate from $+0.11$ to
$+0.03$ and the lower CI bound from $+0.01$ to $-0.04$; the HPO
symmetry test (P7.6 HPO-best, $n=9$) gives $+0.06$, CI still
including zero. The broader broadband bin (P7.8 full ladder $n=24$)
drives it negative.

\textbf{Group B (P7.6 PDE-only, P7.8 full ladder): point estimate
goes negative.} As the broadband bin expands (\fhn{}, \ac{},
burgers\_shock on the PDE side; \fhn{} on the ODE side), $\dbroad$
grows from $+0.04$ at $n=18$ to $+0.15$ at $n=24$ while $\dsmooth$
holds at $+0.07$, inverting the statistic from $+0.03$ to $-0.08$.
The $n=24$ CI includes zero but the direction has flipped; the
PDE-only restriction ($n=9$) shows the same sign in isolation,
$\ddiff = -0.05$. Figure~\ref{fig:cross-task} shows the two PRIMARY
verdicts side-by-side; Figures~\ref{fig:hpo}
and~\ref{fig:hpo-heatmap} give the symmetric-HPO point-estimate
summary and full $3\times 2$ heatmap.

\paragraph{The A12 broadband-expansion decision tree.}
The $n=18 \to 24$ bin expansion was not post-hoc sign tuning but a
mechanical application of the pre-reg \S 4 hardness-ladder definition;
Figure~\ref{fig:sample-size} visualizes the resulting solver-verdict
sensitivity. The steps:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \emph{The pre-registered ladder.} Pre-reg \S 4 lists eight
        systems with explicit regime tags: smooth/periodic for \lv{},
        \vdp{}, heat, burgers smooth; broadband/multiscale for Lorenz,
        \fhn{}, AC, burgers shock.
  \item \emph{The original $n=18$ analysis.} \lv{}, \vdp{}, Lorenz on
        the ODE side; heat, AC, burgers shock on the PDE side. \fhn{}
        was deferred for compute; burgers smooth because its pre-reg
        tag was added during a P7.6 audit. The broadband bin held six
        cells (Lorenz, AC) plus burgers shock, then mis-marked
        smooth/periodic.
  \item \emph{What A12 changed.} Two pre-reg accounting corrections:
        (i) burgers shock re-classified broadband per its
        hyperbolic-conservation-law structure; (ii) \fhn{} added to
        complete ODE coverage at compute already paid. The broadband
        bin grew $n_\mathrm{b} = 6 \to 12$; the smooth bin held at
        $n_\mathrm{s} = 12$; $n_\mathrm{total}$ went 18 to 24.
  \item \emph{Effect on the estimate.} The new \fhn{} cells carried
        $\Delta = +0.51, +0.39, +0.59$ (broadband, quantum-favored;
        \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} edge); the re-classified burgers shock
        cells $\Delta = -0.06, -0.07, +0.13$ (mixed; cPINN-favored on
        s0, s1). $\dbroad$ shifted $+0.04 \to +0.15$, $\dsmooth$
        stayed $+0.07$, and $\dsmooth - \dbroad$ moved $+0.03 \to
        -0.08$.
  \item \emph{Could it have been gerrymandered?} No. The ladder is
        locked at the pre-rigor commit; the steps above are
        mechanical. The PRE\_REG\_AMENDMENT log records both edits
        (A12, A13) with before/after $\dbroad$, gated by
        \texttt{verify\_paper\_integrity}. No ladder configuration
        recovers the original positive sign without removing a
        broadband cell --- and the only candidate, burgers shock, was
        added by A12, not removed.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Group C (P5 forecaster, P7.10 forecaster combined +
quantum-isolated): solidly negative, CI excludes zero.} Both the
pre-reg-mandated combined verdict ($\ddiff = -0.50$, CI $[-0.80,
-0.24]$) and the quantum-isolated decomposition ($\ddiff = -0.62$, CI
$[-1.17, -0.18]$) significantly reject the smooth-favoring direction
of $H_1$. The liquid-isolated decomposition is the only verdict whose
point estimate matches the Schuld--Fourier
prediction~\cite{schuld2021effect}: $\ddiff = +0.12$, CI $[-0.10,
+0.38]$ (includes zero; underpowered at $n=9$).

\subsection{Decision-rule application}
\label{sec:verdict-decision}

Pre-reg \S7 specifies the mechanical decision rule. PRIMARY SOLVER
(P7.8 full ladder $n=24$): $\ddiff = -0.084$, CI $[-0.278, +0.061]$
includes zero $\Rightarrow$ $H_1$ \textbf{FALSIFIED}. PRIMARY
FORECASTER (P7.10 combined $n=9$): $\ddiff = -0.501$, CI $[-0.804,
-0.244]$ excludes zero in the \emph{negative} direction $\Rightarrow$
$H_1$ \textbf{FALSIFIED}, the regime contrast running opposite to the
pre-registered prediction.

Both PRIMARY verdicts FALSIFY $H_1$, the forecaster significantly
against the Schuld--Fourier direction. Per pre-reg \S7 this outcome
is independently publishable: the program was built to deliver an
honest CONFIRMED-or-FALSIFIED result, and the FALSIFIED result is the
scientific contribution.

\subsection{Power and minimum-detectable-effect}
\label{sec:verdict-power}

The two PRIMARY verdicts have very different statistical reaches.
On the SOLVER side ($n=24$ post-A12) the paired-bootstrap 95\% CI
half-width on $\ddiff$ is $\approx 0.17$. Reading the half-width as a
proxy for the minimum detectable effect at $\alpha = 0.05$, the
solver verdict is powered for $|\Delta| \gtrsim 0.17$ and
underpowered below. The observed $\hat{\Delta}_{\text{solver}} =
-0.084$ sits inside the underpowered band; ``FALSIFIED with CI
crossing zero'' is the honest statement that the data are compatible
with a small effect of either sign. On the FORECASTER side ($n=9$)
the half-width is $\approx 0.28$, but $\hat{\Delta}_{\text{combined}}
= -0.501$ exceeds it by a wide margin --- the negative exclusion of
zero is no knife-edge call. The asymmetry is intentional: under
pre-reg \S 4 the solver contrast is more expensive per cell (one
optimization per system) than the forecaster contrast (one rollout
per family per system), so the budget favored the forecaster's
tighter power. Phase~C of the roadmap (Appendix~A) doubles the
per-family solver sample to $n=48$, sharpening the minimum detectable
effect to $|\Delta_{\min}| \approx 0.12$.

\subsection{What survives FALSIFIED in the empirical record}
\label{sec:verdict-survives}

The negative aggregate coexists with three positive sub-findings
independent of the regime contrast.

\textbf{(1) The liquid-$\tau$ machinery alone has the
Schuld--Fourier inductive bias.} The P7.10 liquid-isolated verdict
($\ddiff = +0.12$, CI $[-0.10, +0.38]$) is the only one matching the
pre-registered direction: the classical LTC forecaster has a small
smooth-favoring contribution that the quantum circuit destroys ---
directly relevant to the liquid-neural-network
literature~\cite{hasani2021liquid,hasani2022closed}.

\textbf{(2) Family-specific quantum advantages exist on stiff
fast--slow dynamics.} \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} attains $\relltwo =
0.327, 0.356, 0.329$ on \fhn{} (24$\times$ tighter
seed-standard-deviation than the matched classical PINN; mean error
$2.6\times$ lower; Section~\ref{sec:solver-fhn}). On the forecaster
task, \texttt{rf\_qrc} takes the strongest Lorenz cells ($\relltwo =
0.61, 0.46$ vs classical-LTC $0.86, 1.65$;
Section~\ref{sec:forecaster-cells}). The $H_1$ falsification does not
contradict these; they sit beneath the aggregate as mechanism
datapoints.

\textbf{(3) Symmetric HPO does not rescue any quantum advantage.}
The P7.6 HPO-best verdict (both sides tuned at three anchor cells)
returns the same FALSIFIED outcome as the default-Adam $n=18$
verdict, with a similar estimate ($+0.06$ vs $+0.03$). The
Bowles--Schuld ``tune both sides''~\cite{bowles2024better}
sensitivity is applied and returns the same conclusion.

\subsection{What FALSIFIED means and does not mean here}
\label{sec:verdict-meaning}

The pre-registered $H_1$ is a claim about the \emph{regime contrast}
$\dsmooth - \dbroad$, not about absolute quantum-vs-classical
performance on any individual system. The FALSIFICATION says exactly:
across the hardness ladder, the regime-conditioned advantage gap that
the Schuld--Fourier argument predicts is absent at matched-capacity
training. It does \emph{not} say:

\begin{itemize}
  \item ``Quantum PINNs are uniformly worse than classical
        PINNs.'' (They are not: \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} clearly
        beats cPINN on \fhn{}.)
  \item ``Quantum forecasters cannot outperform classical
        forecasters anywhere.'' (They can: \texttt{rf\_qrc}
        wins Lorenz s0 and s2.)
  \item ``The quantum-PINN program is uninteresting.'' (The LTC
        decomposition shows the quantum circuit's contribution is the
        active mechanism behind the regime-contrast inversion --- a
        precise mechanistic statement about quantum-PINN inductive
        bias.)
\end{itemize}

The FALSIFIED verdict is a rigorous mechanistic null, and it extends
the Bowles--Schuld~\cite{bowles2024better} program: when a quantum-ML
advantage hypothesis is pre-registered, tested with matched
baselines, symmetric HPO, and an LTC decomposition, and the result is
FALSIFIED, that result is the scientific content. We treat it as
such.
