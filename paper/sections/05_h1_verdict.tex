$^{\dagger}$~\emph{Multiple-comparison control.}~The eleven verdicts
constitute a single inferential family. A Holm--Bonferroni
correction at family-wise $\alpha = 0.05$ (i.e.\ require each
individual CI to exclude zero at $\alpha / m = 0.05/11 \approx
0.0045$) is the strict reading. Under that reading the two PRIMARY
headline verdicts (rows P7.8 and P7.10, bold) are unchanged: the
P7.8 solver CI already crosses zero at uncorrected $\alpha = 0.05$,
so multiple-comparison correction does not affect the FALSIFIED
declaration; the P7.10 forecaster CI excludes zero by
$|\Delta| - \mathrm{|CI_{nearest}|} = |{-}0.501| - |{-}0.244| =
0.257$, comfortably outside the strict-corrected
$\alpha = 0.0045$ envelope under standard
percentile-bootstrap-to-$p$-value translation. The eight ancillary
falsified verdicts hold their FALSIFIED designation independent of
the correction (all crossed zero already); only the supersession
note about the P7.5 raw row is affected, and that row was already
flagged as superseded for unrelated reasons. The pre-registration
\S 7 declaration that the verdict family is a falsification family
(not a discovery family) means Holm--Bonferroni is the appropriate
choice; a more aggressive False Discovery Rate
(Benjamini--Hochberg)~\cite{benjamini1995controlling} control
would only relax the threshold further. The paired-bootstrap
percentile interval underlying every row follows the
construction in Efron and Tibshirani~\cite{efron1993introduction};
we use percentile rather than BCa because the bias correction
estimate is itself noisy at $n{\le}24$, and the multiple-data-set
comparison framework of Dem\v{s}ar~\cite{demsar2006statistical}
informs the family-wise vs.\ FDR choice.

The structure of the table follows a deliberate methodological
escalation. Each row strengthens at least one dimension over the
row above it: sample size ($n=9 \to 18 \to 24$), HPO symmetry
(no $\to$ both sides), baseline coverage (cPINN only $\to$ +LTC
decomposition), and task breadth (ODE-only $\to$ ODE+PDE $\to$
forecaster decomposition). The eight non-raw rows all return the
same qualitative outcome: FALSIFIED.

\subsection{Sign-flip behavior across the layered points}
\label{sec:verdict-signflip}

The verdicts split into three groups by point-estimate sign.

\textbf{Group A (P7.5 raw, P7.6 default-Adam, P7.6 HPO-best):
modest positive $\ddiff$.} These three verdicts have point
estimates in $[+0.03, +0.11]$, with the only CI-excludes-zero
result being P7.5's sample-size-fragile $n=9$ value. Reading
across the rows: doubling the sample from $n=9$ to $n=18$ moves
the point estimate from $+0.11$ to $+0.03$ and brings the lower
CI bound from $+0.01$ to $-0.04$. The HPO symmetry test
(P7.6 HPO-best at $n=9$) gives $+0.06$, still with CI including
zero. Adding the broader broadband-bin coverage (P7.8 full
ladder $n=24$) drives the point estimate negative.

\textbf{Group B (P7.6 PDE-only, P7.8 full ladder): point estimate
goes negative.} As the broadband bin is expanded
(\fhn{}, \ac{}, burgers\_shock added on the PDE side; \fhn{} on
the ODE side), $\dbroad$ grows from $+0.04$ at $n=18$ to $+0.15$
at $n=24$, while $\dsmooth$ stays at $+0.07$. The point estimate
inverts: from $+0.03$ at $n=18$ to $-0.08$ at $n=24$. The CI
includes zero at $n=24$ but the direction has clearly flipped.
The PDE-only restriction ($n=9$) shows the same sign in
isolation: $\ddiff = -0.05$.

\paragraph{The A12 broadband-expansion decision tree, step by step.}
The bin expansion from $n=18$ to $n=24$ was not a post-hoc tuning
to flip a sign; it was a mechanical application of the pre-reg
\S 4 hardness-ladder definition. The decision tree was:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \emph{What is the pre-registered ladder?} Pre-reg \S 4 lists
        eight systems (four ODEs + four PDEs) with explicit regime
        tags (smooth/periodic for \lv{}, \vdp{}, heat, burgers smooth;
        broadband/multiscale for Lorenz, \fhn{}, AC, burgers shock).
  \item \emph{What was the original $n=18$ analysis?} Three ODE
        systems on the ODE side (\lv{}, \vdp{}, Lorenz) plus three
        PDE systems on the PDE side (heat, AC, burgers shock). \fhn{}
        was deferred for compute budget; burgers smooth was deferred
        because its pre-reg tag was added during a P7.6 audit. The
        broadband bin contained six cells (3 seeds $\times$ 2 systems:
        Lorenz on ODE, AC on PDE) and one system (burgers shock) we
        marked as smooth/periodic at the time.
  \item \emph{What did A12 change?} A12 corrected two pre-reg
        accounting errors. (i) burgers shock was re-classified as
        broadband per its hyperbolic-conservation-law structure;
        (ii) \fhn{} was added to complete the ODE side coverage at
        the same compute we'd already paid. The broadband bin grew
        from $n_\mathrm{b} = 6$ to $n_\mathrm{b} = 12$; the smooth
        bin from $n_\mathrm{s} = 12$ to $n_\mathrm{s} = 12$ (no
        change). $n_\mathrm{total}$ went from 18 to 24.
  \item \emph{What was the bin-expansion's effect on the estimate?}
        The new \fhn{} cells carried $\Delta = +0.51, +0.39, +0.59$
        (broadband, quantum-favored; \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} edge);
        the re-classified burgers shock cells carried
        $\Delta = -0.06, -0.07, +0.13$ (broadband, mixed; cPINN
        favored on s0 and s1). The mean shift moved $\dbroad$ from
        $+0.04$ to $+0.15$, while $\dsmooth$ stayed at $+0.07$. The
        statistic $\dsmooth - \dbroad$ moved from $+0.03$ to $-0.08$.
  \item \emph{Could the bin expansion have been gerrymandered?} The
        pre-reg ladder is fixed (locked at the pre-rigor commit); the
        decision tree above is mechanical. The PRE\_REG\_AMENDMENT
        log records both edits (A12, A13) with the empirical
        $\dbroad$ before and after each amendment, gated by the
        \texttt{verify\_paper\_integrity} script. There is no
        configuration of the ladder that recovers the original
        positive sign without removing one of the broadband cells —
        and the only candidate (burgers shock) was added by A12,
        not removed.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Group C (P5 forecaster, P7.10 forecaster combined +
quantum-isolated): point estimate is solidly negative with CI
excluding zero.} The two forecaster verdicts cross the
significance threshold in the negative direction. Both the
pre-reg-mandated combined verdict ($\ddiff = -0.50$,
CI $[-0.80, -0.24]$) and the quantum-isolated decomposition
($\ddiff = -0.62$, CI $[-1.17, -0.18]$) significantly reject
the smooth-favoring direction of $H_1$. The liquid-isolated
decomposition is the only verdict in the entire table whose
point estimate matches the original Schuld--Fourier
prediction~\cite{schuld2021effect}: $\ddiff = +0.12$,
CI $[-0.10, +0.38]$ (CI includes zero; underpowered at $n=9$).

\subsection{Decision-rule application}
\label{sec:verdict-decision}

Pre-reg \S7 specifies the mechanical decision rule for both
tasks. The PRIMARY SOLVER verdict (P7.8 full ladder $n=24$):
$\ddiff = -0.084$, CI $[-0.278, +0.061]$ includes zero $\Rightarrow$
$H_1$ \textbf{FALSIFIED}. The PRIMARY FORECASTER verdict
(P7.10 combined $n=9$): $\ddiff = -0.501$, CI $[-0.804, -0.244]$
excludes zero in the \emph{negative} direction $\Rightarrow$
$H_1$ \textbf{FALSIFIED} (the regime contrast goes the opposite
way from the pre-registered prediction).

Both PRIMARY verdicts FALSIFY $H_1$. The forecaster's combined
verdict additionally points the opposite way from the
Schuld--Fourier prediction, with statistical significance. Per
pre-reg \S7, this outcome is independently publishable: the
program was set up to deliver an honest CONFIRMED-or-FALSIFIED
result, and the FALSIFIED result is the scientific contribution.

\subsection{Power and minimum-detectable-effect}
\label{sec:verdict-power}

The two PRIMARY verdicts have very different statistical reaches.
On the SOLVER side ($n=24$ post-A12), the paired-bootstrap 95\%
CI half-width on $\ddiff$ is $\approx 0.17$. Taking the
CI half-width as a proxy for the minimum effect $|\Delta_{\min}|$
that the sample could have rejected $H_0$ against at $\alpha =
0.05$, the solver verdict is comfortably powered to detect
effects of $|\Delta| \gtrsim 0.17$ and underpowered for effects
smaller than that. The observed point estimate
$\hat{\Delta}_{\text{solver}} = -0.084$ sits inside the
underpowered band; the verdict ``FALSIFIED with CI crossing
zero'' is the honest statement that the data are compatible with
both a small positive effect and a small negative effect.
On the FORECASTER side ($n=9$), the CI half-width is
$\approx 0.28$ but the observed $\hat{\Delta}_{\text{combined}}
= -0.501$ exceeds it by a wide margin: the verdict's exclusion
of zero negatively is not a knife-edge call --- it would survive
substantially larger CI inflation than the actual bootstrap
returns. The asymmetry is intentional: under pre-reg \S 4 the
solver-task contrast is more expensive per cell (one optimization
per ODE/PDE system) than the forecaster-task contrast (one
rollout per family per system), so the $n$-budget allocation
favored the forecaster verdict's tighter power. A wider solver
sample would address the underpowered band: Phase~C of the
roadmap (Appendix~A) doubles the per-family solver sample to
$n=48$, sharpening the smallest detectable effect to
$|\Delta_{\min}| \approx 0.12$.

\subsection{What survives FALSIFIED in the empirical record}
\label{sec:verdict-survives}

The negative aggregate verdict coexists with three substantive
positive sub-findings that are independent of the regime
contrast itself.

\textbf{(1) The liquid-$\tau$ machinery alone has the
Schuld--Fourier inductive bias.} The P7.10 liquid-isolated
verdict ($\ddiff = +0.12$, CI $[-0.10, +0.38]$) is the only
verdict in the table whose point estimate matches the
pre-registered prediction direction. The classical LTC
(non-quantum) forecaster has a small regime-favoring-smooth
contribution that the quantum circuit destroys. This is
directly relevant to the broader liquid-neural-network
literature~\cite{wang2022causality}.

\textbf{(2) Family-specific quantum advantages exist on stiff
fast--slow dynamics.} \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} attains $\relltwo
= 0.327, 0.356, 0.329$ on \fhn{} (twenty-four times tighter seed
variance than the matched classical PINN; mean error $2.6\times$
lower; Section~\ref{sec:solver-fhn}). On the forecaster task,
\texttt{rf\_qrc} achieves the strongest Lorenz cells
($\relltwo = 0.61, 0.46$ vs classical-LTC $0.86, 1.65$;
Section~\ref{sec:forecaster-cells}). These are positive
regime-specific findings that the $H_1$ falsification does not
contradict; they sit beneath the aggregate verdict as observed
mechanism datapoints.

\textbf{(3) Symmetric HPO does not rescue any quantum
advantage on the regime contrast.} The P7.6 HPO-best verdict
(both sides tuned at three anchor cells) returns the same
FALSIFIED outcome as the default-Adam $n=18$ verdict, with a
similar point estimate ($+0.06$ vs $+0.03$). The Bowles--Schuld
``tune both sides''~\cite{bowles2024better} sensitivity is
explicitly applied and explicitly returns the same conclusion.

\subsection{What FALSIFIED means and does not mean here}
\label{sec:verdict-meaning}

The pre-registered $H_1$ is a specific claim about the
\emph{regime contrast} $\dsmooth - \dbroad$, not a claim about
absolute quantum-vs-classical performance on any individual
system. The FALSIFICATION says exactly: across the
pre-registered hardness ladder, the regime-conditioned advantage
gap that the Schuld--Fourier theoretical argument predicts is
not present at matched-capacity training. It does \emph{not}
say:

\begin{itemize}
  \item ``Quantum PINNs are uniformly worse than classical
        PINNs.'' (They are not: \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} clearly
        beats cPINN on \fhn{}.)
  \item ``Quantum forecasters cannot outperform classical
        forecasters anywhere.'' (They can: \texttt{rf\_qrc}
        wins Lorenz s0 and s2.)
  \item ``The quantum-PINN program is uninteresting.'' (The LTC
        decomposition reveals that the quantum circuit's
        contribution is the active mechanism behind the
        regime-contrast inversion -- which is itself a
        precise mechanistic statement about quantum-PINN
        inductive bias.)
\end{itemize}

The FALSIFIED verdict is a rigorous mechanistic null on a
specific hypothesis. It directly extends the
Bowles--Schuld~\cite{bowles2024better} methodological program:
when a quantum-ML advantage hypothesis is pre-registered, tested
with matched baselines and symmetric HPO and an LTC
decomposition, and the result is FALSIFIED, that result is the
scientific content. We treat it as such.
