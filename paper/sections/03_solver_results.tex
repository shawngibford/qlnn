\section{Solver-task results}
\label{sec:solver}

The solver task is the pre-registered gating task for H1
(Section~\ref{sec:methods-verdict}): each model fits a single
trajectory by physics-residual minimization, and we record the
relative-$L^2$ error of the trained model against a high-resolution
reference field. This section reports the full $n=24$ per-cell
matrix and the four solver-task sensitivity points.

\subsection{Per-cell error matrix (all-to-all)}
\label{sec:solver-cells}

Table~\ref{tab:cell-matrix} reports, for each (system, seed)
cell, the relative-$L^2$ error of \emph{every} quantum-PINN
family in our roster (Section~\ref{sec:methods-roster}) alongside
the matched classical PINN baseline -- the full all-to-all
contrast matrix. The best-per-row quantum family is bolded; the
$\Delta$ column compares the best-QLNN to the cPINN on that row.
The architectural family names abbreviate to: ``cheb'' for
\texttt{chebyshev\_dqc} (or \texttt{\_2d} on the PDE half),
``fnn'' for \texttt{te\_qpinn\_fnn(\_2d)}, ``qnn'' for
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn(\_2d)}, and ``qcp'' for
\texttt{qcpinn(\_2d)}.

No single quantum family dominates the matrix: \texttt{qcpinn}
wins on \lv{} and most PDE-smooth cells; \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn}
dominates the broadband bin (Lorenz, \fhn{}, \ac{}); and
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_fnn} wins a handful of edge cells
(\vdp{} s0,s2, heat s2, burgers\_smooth s1, burgers\_shock s2).
\texttt{chebyshev\_dqc} is never the per-row winner on the
$n=24$ matrix in its 2D PDE form (its smooth-only
logistic-saturation feature map sits at
$\relltwo \approx 0.42$--$0.44$ on burgers\_shock and similar
on AC). \texttt{qcpinn} is the largest classical-parameter
ansatz in the roster (Section~\ref{sec:methods-roster}) and
its dominance on \lv{} should be read with that capacity-overhead
caveat in mind.

The all-to-all view exposes structural family preferences invisible
in the single best-QLNN reduction. Three patterns stand out.

\textbf{(1) Family specialization is real.} \texttt{qcpinn} owns the
smooth, very-easy-to-fit cells (\lv{}, heat, burgers\_smooth s0)
where its 706-classical-parameter overhead presumably helps; the
pure-quantum \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} (zero classical parameters)
owns the broadband bin entirely. The two TE-QPINN variants
sometimes trade leadership by seed on stiff cells (\vdp{},
burgers\_shock).

\textbf{(2) Lorenz is a shared-failure datapoint.} At $T=2$
($\approx 2$ Lyapunov times) every quantum family and the cPINN
sit at the predict-zero baseline ($\relltwo \approx 0.98$--$1.00$).
The $\Delta \approx +0.02$ contribution to the verdict is in the
noise. We treat the Lorenz cells as discriminating evidence for
neither H1 nor its inverse.

\textbf{(3) chebyshev\_dqc and qcpinn fail on different broadband
PDEs.} \texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} sits at $\relltwo \approx 0.42$--$0.44$
on all three burgers\_shock seeds (smooth-only feature map cannot
represent the near-shock gradient) and at $\relltwo \approx 0.52$--$0.62$
on AC (cannot represent the sharp two-front structure).
\texttt{qcpinn\_2d} fails on AC ($\relltwo \approx 0.49$--$0.90$)
even with its classical-parameter overhead, but it is the leader
on burgers\_shock (s0, s1). These family-level failure modes are
the kind of structural information the best-QLNN reduction
discards.

The smooth-bin mean $\dsmooth = +0.067$ and broad-bin mean
$\dbroad = +0.152$. The broadband mean is dominated by the
\fhn{} and \ac{} (s=0) cells; the Lorenz cells contribute
$\Delta \approx +0.02$ each because both quantum and classical
collapse to the predict-zero baseline at $T = 2$ Lyapunov times
($\relltwo \approx 0.98$).

\subsection{Per-system difficulty profile}
\label{sec:per-system-profile}

The aggregate ``smooth-vs-broadband'' bin masks substantial
within-regime variance. We summarize the dominant inductive challenge
per system, why it is hard or easy, and which family handles it best.
Figures~\ref{fig:system-heatmap} and~\ref{fig:delta-distribution}
provide the per-cell support for this narrative.

\begin{description}
  \item[\lv{} (S, predator-prey).] Periodic limit cycle in 2D with
        smooth derivatives; the Schuld--Fourier feature
        space~\cite{schuld2021effect} captures it almost exactly
        at $K_{\max} = 3$. All four quantum families and the cPINN
        attain $\relltwo \le 0.02$; \texttt{qcpinn} leads
        ($\relltwo \approx 0.007$). The cell carries small positive
        $\Delta$ contributions, mostly from cPINN underfit at lower
        learning rates.
  \item[\vdp{} (S, limit cycle with relaxation).] Stiff oscillation
        with a slow drift + fast snap-back. The relaxation phase is
        a localized derivative discontinuity that the smooth
        Fourier series cannot represent at $K_{\max} = 3$; both
        quantum and classical sides plateau around
        $\relltwo \approx 0.18$--$0.34$. \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn}
        is the most consistent on this regime, with the lowest
        seed variance.
  \item[Heat (S, diffusion).] Linear PDE with analytic
        Fourier-mode decomposition; the Schuld--Fourier ansatz is a
        natural fit. All families attain $\relltwo \le 0.005$;
        \texttt{qcpinn} and \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} are
        essentially tied at the integrity-gate floor. This is the
        easiest cell in the entire matrix.
  \item[Burgers smooth (S, parabolic with mild nonlinearity).]
        Smooth dissipation around a coherent traveling profile;
        $\nu = 0.07$ keeps gradients moderate. All quantum
        families attain $\relltwo \le 0.045$; cPINN attains
        $\le 0.025$. Marginally cPINN-favored at every seed —
        the cleanest single counterexample to the
        smooth-favors-quantum hypothesis in the matrix.
  \item[\fhn{} (B, fast-slow stiff).] Excitable membrane with two
        timescales differing by $\approx 12.5\times$. The fast-snap
        recovery phase is the canonical stiff-PINN failure
        mode~\cite{krishnapriyan2021characterizing,wang2022whenwhy}
        and explains the cPINN's catastrophic seed-2 cell
        ($\relltwo = 1.326$ vs the cPINN seed-mean of 0.870).
        \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} carries the positive sub-finding
        here: $\relltwo = 0.327, 0.356, 0.329$ with a $24\times$
        tighter seed variance.
  \item[Allen--Cahn (B, double-well front).] Sharp interfaces
        between $\pm 1$ basins; the smooth Fourier feature space
        cannot represent the front structure at $K_{\max} = 3$.
        Both \texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} and \texttt{qcpinn\_2d}
        sit at $\relltwo \approx 0.49$--$0.90$ depending on seed.
        \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn\_2d} is the lone non-failing
        family here.
  \item[Burgers shock (B, hyperbolic with steep gradient).] Same
        front-representation challenge as Allen--Cahn but with
        rightward-propagating dynamics; cPINN handles it best
        ($\relltwo \le 0.05$ at all seeds) because its dense MLP
        capacity covers the locally-piecewise structure.
  \item[Lorenz (B, chaotic).] Strange-attractor dynamics on
        $T = 2$ ($\approx 2$ Lyapunov times). At this horizon the
        trial solution's hard-IC structure cannot diverge from
        $u(0)$ enough to follow the chaotic trajectory; everything
        collapses to the predict-zero baseline
        ($\relltwo \approx 0.98$). The Lorenz contribution to the
        verdict is in the noise.
\end{description}

The takeaway: of the eight systems, exactly one (\fhn{}) shows a
clear regime-specific quantum-PINN structural advantage, and exactly
one (Burgers smooth) shows a clear regime-specific classical-PINN
advantage. The remaining six are shared-failure or shared-success
cells. This is precisely the per-cell distribution the verdict-level
$\dsmooth - \dbroad$ statistic compresses into a single number — and
the n=24 PRIMARY CI crosses zero because the per-cell distribution
itself is not concentrated.

\label{sec:solver-verdicts}

(Section~\ref{sec:methods-verdict}) at four successively-stronger
sample-size and tuning configurations on the solver task; all
four return the same qualitative outcome (Table~\ref{tab:verdicts}).

appears in the P7.5 commit -- is the only configuration whose

to push the lower CI bound to $-0.04$; at $n=24$ (row~4) the
point estimate flips sign.

\subsection{The sign flip and the \fhn{} positive sub-finding}
\label{sec:solver-fhn}

The sign flip from $\ddiff = +0.032$ at $n=18$ to $\ddiff =
-0.084$ at $n=24$ is driven entirely by the new broadband
cells, with $\dbroad$ moving from $+0.036$ at $n=18$ to $+0.152$
at $n=24$ ($\dsmooth$ is unchanged at $+0.067$ because the same
12 smooth cells contribute both verdicts). The two largest
single-cell quantum advantages in the matrix sit at
\fhn{} seed~2 ($\Delta = +0.998$) and \ac{} seed~0
($\Delta = +0.149$).

The \fhn{} cells warrant special discussion. The
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} ansatz attains
$\relltwo = 0.327, 0.356, 0.329$ across the three seeds, a
mean of $0.337$ and a sample standard deviation of $0.016$.
The matched classical PINN attains $0.640, 0.641, 1.326$
($\mathrm{mean} = 0.870$, $\mathrm{std} = 0.396$), with seed~2
exhibiting a catastrophic training failure
($\relltwo > 1$ indicates worse than predict-zero). The
quantum-side seed variance is roughly twenty-four times tighter,
and the mean is roughly two-and-a-half times lower. Notably the
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} architecture has zero classical
parameters by construction (Section~\ref{sec:methods-roster}),
so this is a clean structural quantum win rather than a
classical-overhead artifact.

We treat \fhn{} as a regime-specific positive
sub-finding within an otherwise-falsified main verdict: the
combination of a stiff fast--slow nullcline and a 2D state
space appears to be a class of dynamics on which the
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} hypothesis class is structurally
well-matched. Whether this generalizes beyond \fhn{} is left as
follow-up work (the pre-reg's Kuramoto system would test the
same hypothesis class at higher state dimension, deferred per
A11).

\subsection{Family-of-models failure modes}
\label{sec:solver-failures}

Two family-level failure modes are visible in the matrix.

\textbf{\texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} on burgers\_shock and AC.}
Across all three burgers\_shock seeds the
\texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} ansatz achieves $\relltwo \approx
0.42$--$0.44$, substantially worse than the other three quantum
families on the same cells and worse than the matched cPINN
(which achieves $\approx 0.13$--$0.27$). The Chebyshev tower
feature map's polynomial coordinate encoding is a poor match
for the near-shock sharp gradient at $t^* \approx 1$. The
same family is also outperformed by \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn\_2d}
on AC. We document this as a confirmation of the
chebyshev family's smooth-only specialization observed in our
earlier solver-demo work.

\textbf{All four families on Lorenz at $T=2$.} Every quantum
family and the classical PINN collapse to the predict-zero
baseline on Lorenz ($\relltwo \approx 0.98$ for all twelve
Lorenz cells). The pre-registered Lorenz horizon is
$T = 2 \approx 2$ Lyapunov times; this is the regime where
the system has begun to diverge from its initial trajectory
but the trial solution's hard-IC structure cannot yet diverge
itself. The Lorenz contribution to the verdict is near-zero
in all four sensitivity points; we treat the Lorenz cells as a
shared-failure datapoint rather than as discriminating evidence
for or against H1.
