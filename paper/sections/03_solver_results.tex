\section{Solver-task results}
\label{sec:solver}

The solver task is the pre-registered gating task for H1
(Section~\ref{sec:methods-verdict}): each model fits a single
trajectory by physics-residual minimization, scored by relative-$L^2$
error against a high-resolution reference. We report the full $n=24$
per-cell matrix and the four solver-task sensitivity points.

\subsection{Per-cell error matrix (all-to-all)}
\label{sec:solver-cells}

Table~\ref{tab:cell-matrix} reports, for each (system, seed)
cell, the relative-$L^2$ error of \emph{every} quantum-PINN
family in our roster (Section~\ref{sec:methods-roster}) alongside
the matched classical PINN baseline. The best-per-row quantum family is
bolded; the $\Delta$ column compares the best-QLNN to the cPINN on that
row. Family names abbreviate to ``cheb'' for \texttt{chebyshev\_dqc}
(\texttt{\_2d} on the PDE half), ``fnn'' for
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_fnn(\_2d)}, ``qnn'' for
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn(\_2d)}, and ``qcp'' for \texttt{qcpinn(\_2d)};
the families are summarized in Figure~\ref{fig:circuit-zoo} and
visualized per-cell in Figure~\ref{fig:solver-multistate} and
per-system in Figure~\ref{fig:trajectory-grid}.

No single quantum family dominates: \texttt{qcpinn}
wins on \lv{} and most PDE-smooth cells; \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn}
dominates the broadband bin (Lorenz, \fhn{}, \ac{}); and
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_fnn} wins a handful of edge cells
(\vdp{} s0,s2, heat s2, burgers\_smooth s1, burgers\_shock s2).
\texttt{chebyshev\_dqc} is never a per-row winner in its 2D PDE form
(its smooth-only feature map sits at $\relltwo \approx 0.42$--$0.44$ on
burgers\_shock and similar on AC). Note that \texttt{qcpinn} is the
largest classical-parameter ansatz in the roster, so its \lv{}
dominance carries a capacity-overhead caveat.

The all-to-all view exposes structural family preferences invisible
in the single best-QLNN reduction. Three patterns stand out.

\textbf{(1) Family specialization is real.} \texttt{qcpinn} owns the
easy smooth cells (\lv{}, heat, burgers\_smooth s0) where its
706-classical-parameter overhead presumably helps; the pure-quantum
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} owns the broadband bin entirely. The two
TE-QPINN variants trade leadership by seed on stiff cells (\vdp{},
burgers\_shock).

\textbf{(2) Lorenz is a shared-failure datapoint.} At $T=2$
($\approx 2$ Lyapunov times) every quantum family and the cPINN
sit at the predict-zero baseline ($\relltwo \approx 0.98$--$1.00$);
the $\Delta \approx +0.02$ contribution is in the noise, discriminating
neither H1 nor its inverse.

\textbf{(3) chebyshev\_dqc and qcpinn fail on different broadband
PDEs.} \texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} cannot represent the near-shock
gradient on burgers\_shock ($\relltwo \approx 0.42$--$0.44$) nor the
sharp two-front structure on AC ($\relltwo \approx 0.52$--$0.62$).
\texttt{qcpinn\_2d} fails on AC ($\relltwo \approx 0.49$--$0.90$)
despite its overhead, yet leads on burgers\_shock (s0, s1).

The bin means are $\dsmooth = +0.067$ and $\dbroad = +0.152$, the
latter dominated by the \fhn{} and \ac{} (s=0) cells.

\subsection{Per-system difficulty profile}
\label{sec:per-system-profile}

The aggregate bins mask substantial within-regime variance. We
summarize the dominant inductive challenge per system and which family
handles it best; Figures~\ref{fig:system-heatmap}
and~\ref{fig:delta-distribution} give the per-cell support.

\begin{description}
  \item[\lv{} (S, predator-prey).] Periodic limit cycle in 2D with
        smooth derivatives; the Schuld--Fourier feature
        space~\cite{schuld2021effect} captures it almost exactly
        at $K_{\max} = 3$. All four quantum families and the cPINN
        attain $\relltwo \le 0.02$; \texttt{qcpinn} leads
        ($\relltwo \approx 0.007$). The small positive $\Delta$ comes
        mostly from cPINN underfit at lower learning rates.
  \item[\vdp{} (S, limit cycle with relaxation).] Stiff oscillation
        with a slow drift + fast snap-back. The relaxation phase is
        a localized derivative discontinuity that the smooth
        Fourier series cannot represent at $K_{\max} = 3$; both
        quantum and classical sides plateau around
        $\relltwo \approx 0.18$--$0.34$. \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn}
        is the most consistent on this regime, with the lowest
        seed standard deviation.
  \item[Heat (S, diffusion).] Linear PDE with analytic Fourier-mode
        decomposition --- a natural fit for the Schuld--Fourier ansatz,
        and the easiest cell in the matrix. All families attain
        $\relltwo \le 0.005$; \texttt{qcpinn} and \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn}
        tie at the integrity-gate floor.
  \item[Burgers smooth (S, parabolic with mild nonlinearity).]
        Smooth dissipation around a coherent traveling profile;
        $\nu = 0.07$ keeps gradients moderate. All quantum
        families attain $\relltwo \le 0.045$; cPINN attains
        $\le 0.025$. Marginally cPINN-favored at every seed —
        the cleanest single counterexample to the
        smooth-favors-quantum hypothesis in the matrix.
  \item[\fhn{} (B, fast-slow stiff).] Excitable membrane with two
        timescales differing by $\approx 12.5\times$. The fast-snap
        recovery phase is the canonical stiff-PINN failure
        mode~\cite{krishnapriyan2021characterizing,wang2022whenwhy}
        and explains the cPINN's catastrophic seed-2 cell
        ($\relltwo = 1.326$ vs the cPINN seed-mean of 0.870).
        \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} carries the positive sub-finding
        here: $\relltwo = 0.327, 0.356, 0.329$ with a $24\times$
        tighter seed standard deviation.
  \item[Allen--Cahn (B, double-well front).] Sharp interfaces
        between $\pm 1$ basins; the smooth Fourier feature space
        cannot represent the front structure at $K_{\max} = 3$.
        Both \texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} and \texttt{qcpinn\_2d}
        sit at $\relltwo \approx 0.49$--$0.90$ depending on seed.
        \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn\_2d} is the lone non-failing
        family here.
  \item[Burgers shock (B, hyperbolic with steep gradient).] Same
        front-representation challenge as Allen--Cahn but with
        rightward-propagating dynamics; cPINN handles it best
        ($\relltwo \le 0.05$ at all seeds) because its dense MLP
        capacity covers the locally-piecewise structure.
  \item[Lorenz (B, chaotic).] Strange-attractor dynamics on
        $T = 2$ ($\approx 2$ Lyapunov times). At this horizon the
        trial solution's hard-IC structure cannot diverge from
        $u(0)$ enough to follow the chaotic trajectory, so everything
        collapses to the predict-zero baseline
        ($\relltwo \approx 0.98$); the contribution to the verdict is
        in the noise.
\end{description}

Figure~\ref{fig:system-spectrum} shows each system's Fourier amplitude
content relative to the accessible bandwidth $K_{\max} = 3$. Of the
eight systems, exactly one (\fhn{}) shows a clear regime-specific
quantum-PINN advantage and exactly one (Burgers smooth) a clear
classical-PINN advantage; the remaining six are shared-failure or
shared-success cells. The $n=24$ PRIMARY CI crosses zero precisely
because this per-cell distribution is not concentrated.

\subsection{The sign flip and the \fhn{} positive sub-finding}
\label{sec:solver-fhn}

The sign flip from $\ddiff = +0.032$ at $n=18$ to $\ddiff =
-0.084$ at $n=24$ is driven entirely by the new broadband
cells, with $\dbroad$ moving from $+0.036$ at $n=18$ to $+0.152$
at $n=24$ ($\dsmooth$ is unchanged at $+0.067$ because the same
12 smooth cells contribute both verdicts). The two largest
single-cell quantum advantages in the matrix sit at
\fhn{} seed~2 ($\Delta = +0.998$) and \ac{} seed~0
($\Delta = +0.149$).

The \fhn{} cells warrant special discussion. The
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} ansatz attains
$\relltwo = 0.327, 0.356, 0.329$ (mean $0.337$, std $0.016$),
while the matched classical PINN attains $0.640, 0.641, 1.326$
(mean $0.870$, std $0.396$), seed~2 failing catastrophically
($\relltwo > 1$ is worse than predict-zero). The quantum side is
roughly twenty-four times tighter in seed std and two-and-a-half times
lower in mean. Because \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn} has zero classical
parameters by construction (Section~\ref{sec:methods-roster}), this is
a clean structural quantum win, not a classical-overhead artifact.

We treat \fhn{} as a regime-specific positive sub-finding within an
otherwise-falsified verdict: a stiff fast--slow nullcline in a 2D state
space appears structurally well-matched to the \texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn}
hypothesis class. Whether this generalizes is left to follow-up work
(the pre-reg's Kuramoto system would test it at higher state dimension,
deferred per A11).

\subsection{Family-of-models failure modes}
\label{sec:solver-failures}

Two family-level failure modes are visible in the matrix.

\textbf{\texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} on burgers\_shock and AC.}
On all three burgers\_shock seeds \texttt{chebyshev\_dqc\_2d} achieves
$\relltwo \approx 0.42$--$0.44$, well below the other quantum families
and the matched cPINN ($\approx 0.13$--$0.27$): the Chebyshev tower's
polynomial coordinate encoding poorly matches the near-shock gradient
at $t^* \approx 1$. The same family is outperformed by
\texttt{te\_qpinn\_qnn\_2d} on AC, confirming the chebyshev family's
smooth-only specialization seen in our earlier solver-demo work.

\textbf{All four families on Lorenz at $T=2$.} Every quantum family and
the cPINN collapse to the predict-zero baseline ($\relltwo \approx 0.98$
across all twelve Lorenz cells): at $\approx 2$ Lyapunov times the
system has begun to diverge but the trial solution's hard-IC structure
cannot. The contribution to the verdict is near-zero at all four
sensitivity points, so the Lorenz cells are a shared-failure datapoint
rather than discriminating evidence.
